About company

Lamborghini Huracan(Supercar of the Italian sports premium brand Lamborghini, replacing the Gallardo.) Since the beginning of January 2014, Huracan has been featured in 130 closed events that took place in 60 cities. The world premiere was held at the Geneva Motor Show in March 2014. The model was named "Huracan", which, according to the tradition of the Italian manufacturer, is associated with the world of bullfight: such a name was worn by a fighting bull that spoke in the Spanish city of Alicante in 1879.

Briefly about the design

The mid-engined four-wheel drive supercar, based on the carbon-aluminum chassis, which formed the basis of the second-generation Audi R8. The weight of the car is 1,422 kilograms, which is 12 heavier than Gallardo. The weight-to-power ratio is 2.33 kg per horsepower. Supercar is equipped with a modernized 5.2-liter V10 from Gallardo. Power - 610 hp (455 kW) at 8,250 revolutions per minute, and 650 Nm of torque at 6,500 revolutions per minute. Acceleration to 100 is 3.2 seconds, up to 200 - 9.6 seconds, and the maximum speed is equal to 325 kilometers per hour. The average fuel consumption is 12.5 liters per hundred runs. The engine works in tandem with a robotized 7-speed gearbox, dubbed the Lamborghini Doppia Frizione (LDF), an upgraded version of the S-tronic transmission used by the first-generation Audi R8.

Interior

The Huracán has various interior options for more comfort inside the car. It has a choice for full electric control and heating of the sport seats in addition to the standard lengthwise alignment and electric backrest. The car also has an optional navigation system and leather upholstering. The Huracán is one of the first sports cars moving the infotainment system out of the center stack: Lamborghini Infotainment System. The result: a display that lets the driver decide what they want to see. It is the first Lamborghini automobile to use the 12.3 inch TFT virtual cockpit from parent company Audi, which is available with high-definition (1440 x 540) 3D computer graphics, Bluetooth, Google Earth GPS navigation and Multi Media Interface-like menu and dashboard controls, all powered by Nvidia Tegra 3.
